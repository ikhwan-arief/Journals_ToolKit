DOAJ Readiness Checklist – Self-Check Toolkit

Use this tool to review your journal website before applying to the Directory of Open Access Journals (DOAJ). The result is indicative only: DOAJ will always make its own independent decision.

📝 For journal editors – internal pre-evaluation

Journal identity

Fill in your journal details (for your own record only; these fields do not affect the calculation).

Checklist questions

Answer each question based only on what is publicly visible on your journal website (and where indicated, the record for your ISSN at portal.issn.org). If you cannot clearly verify something, the honest answer is No.

Section 1 – Journal identity & basic open access
Core information about the journal’s identity, ISSN(s), and open access status.
1. Journal homepage is publicly accessible.
Required
The main journal URL opens normally in a browser, without login, payment, VPN, or special access.
2. Journal title is clear, consistent, and matches the title in the ISSN Portal.
Required
The same title appears on the homepage, logo/header, and article pages, and it matches the title shown for the journal in portal.issn.org.
3. ISSN(s) are shown on the journal website and match the ISSN Portal.
Required
Print and/or online ISSN(s) are clearly displayed and exactly match the ISSN data (including medium) in the journal’s record at portal.issn.org.
4. All research articles are freely available to read online.
Required
When you open several recent research articles, you can access the full text (HTML and/or PDF) without paying, subscribing, or logging in.
5. There is no embargo period (immediate open access).
Required
Articles are open access immediately when they are published, not after an embargo (e.g. 6 or 12 months). If practice or policy is unclear, answer "No".
6. Articles are presented individually, not only as whole-issue files.
Required
Each article has its own page and/or separate PDF link with its own URL. The journal does not only offer a single PDF per issue without article-level access.
Section 2 – Open access, license & copyright
Clarity and internal consistency of your open access statement, license, and copyright notice.
7. The website clearly states that the journal is open access.
Required
There is a visible statement (e.g. "Open Access", "About the Journal") explaining that all content is freely available to readers and easy to find from the homepage.
8. The journal states a specific license for published articles.
Required
The site names a clearly identifiable license (e.g. "CC BY 4.0", "CC BY-NC 4.0", or a clearly worded publisher license). Vague wording like "free for readers" alone is not enough.
9. License terms are on a public, readable page.
Required
A visitor can click from the homepage or menu to a page (e.g. "Copyright & Licensing", "Open Access Policy") and read the license terms without logging in.
10. It is clear what readers may do with the content.
Required
The license or policy explains permitted uses (e.g. read, download, share, reuse with attribution). A logo alone, without any explanation, is not sufficient.
For 11–16, check three places for several recent research articles: (1) the general copyright / policy page, (2) the article view pages, and (3) the PDFs of those articles.
11. Copyright ownership for articles is clearly stated on the website.
Required
There is a clear statement about who holds copyright on the articles (e.g. "Authors retain copyright" or "Copyright © Publisher"), not only a generic website footer.
12. Website copyright policy matches the article view pages.
Required
Copyright information on article/abstract pages (if any) is consistent with the general copyright policy. There are no contradictions.
13. Website copyright policy matches the article PDFs.
Required
Any copyright text in PDFs (if present) does not contradict the website policy. For example, PDFs do not say "All rights reserved" if the policy says "Authors retain copyright" without explanation.
14. License on article view pages matches the general license policy.
Required
If the policy says that all articles use a particular license (e.g. CC BY 4.0), then the article pages show that same license, not a different one without clear justification.
15. License in the PDFs matches the website and article pages.
Required
Where PDFs include license text or icons, they show the same license that is stated on the website and article pages. There are no mismatches.
16. There are no conflicts between copyright and license across website, article pages, and PDFs.
Required
Taking all three locations together, there is a consistent message. There is no combination like "All rights reserved" next to an open license, and no conflicting owners or terms.
Section 3 – Editorial information, peer review & endogeny
17. The journal has a clear Aims & Scope page.
Required
A public page describes the subject area(s) and the types of articles the journal publishes (e.g. research articles, reviews).
18. The Editorial Team is listed with names, affiliations, and online profiles.
Required
The site lists editors and editorial board members with full names, institutional affiliations, and, for most members, at least one verifiable profile (e.g. ORCID, Google Scholar, Scopus Author ID, institutional page) showing their academic expertise.
19. The journal describes its peer review process.
Required
The site explains how peer review works (e.g. single-blind, double-blind, open peer review), who reviews submissions, and at what stage, in a clearly visible section.
20. The journal clearly states that research submissions are peer reviewed before publication.
Required
There is explicit wording that research articles are peer reviewed before acceptance and publication, not just a vague claim of "review".
21. Instructions for Authors are public and usable.
Required
A public "Instructions for Authors" / "Author Guidelines" page exists with submission steps, formatting rules, and basic editorial policies, accessible without login.
22. The proportion of in-house authors does not exceed 25% according to DOAJ’s rule.
Required
Check endogeny:
• If you publish in issues: for each of the latest two issues, count all research articles and how many have at least one author who is an editor, editorial board member, or reviewer. Each issue must be ≤ 25%.
• If you publish continuously: for the last calendar year (minimum 5 research articles), count how many have at least one author who is an editor, editorial board member, or reviewer. The proportion must be ≤ 25%.
23. Most recent research articles display submitted / accepted / published dates.
Recommended
For a sample of recent research articles, the article page and/or PDF show at least two (ideally all three) of: date submitted, date accepted, date published. This is strongly recommended for transparency.
Section 4 – Publication fees & business model
24. The journal clearly states whether it charges Article Processing Charges (APCs).
Required
The website explicitly states either that the journal does charge APCs or that it does not charge APCs. If this is not clearly stated anywhere, answer "No".
25. If APCs are charged, the exact amount and conditions are clearly shown.
Required
If APCs exist, the website shows the fee (currency and amount) and what it covers. If APCs are charged but the amount is not publicly visible, answer "No". If no APCs are charged and the website clearly states this, you may answer "Yes".
26. Any other author-facing fees are fully described.
Required
The website lists all other possible author charges (e.g. submission fees, page charges, colour printing, print copies) and explains when they apply. Hidden or unclear fees mean "No".
27. If waivers or discounts are offered, they are clearly explained.
Required
If waivers or discounts exist (e.g. for authors from certain countries or with financial hardship), the website describes who is eligible and under what conditions. If they exist but are not described, answer "No".
Section 5 – Research integrity, ethics & misconduct
28. The journal has a clear publication ethics / malpractice statement.
Required
A page explains how the journal handles ethical issues (e.g. plagiarism, data fabrication, duplicate publication), or clearly adopts recognised guidelines and describes how they are applied.
29. There is a visible policy on plagiarism and originality of submissions.
Required
The site states that submissions must be original and not previously published, and ideally mentions whether plagiarism-checking tools are used.
30. The journal has a policy on conflicts of interest and authorship.
Required
The website explains how authors, reviewers, and editors should declare conflicts of interest, and gives rules on who qualifies as an author and how contributions should be acknowledged.
31. There is a clear policy on corrections, retractions, and expressions of concern.
Required
The site describes how the journal will handle serious issues discovered after publication, including corrections, retractions, or expressions of concern, and how these will be labelled.
Section 6 – Quality signals, metrics & indexing
32. The journal does not endorse or display misleading metrics.
Required
The site does not display or promote fake or unverifiable "impact factors", invented indicators, or badges from unknown providers that could mislead authors and readers about the journal’s quality or impact.
33. Indexing and ranking claims are accurate and verifiable.
Required
When the journal claims to be indexed or ranked in specific services, these claims can be checked and confirmed on the official sites of those services. Outdated, exaggerated, or unverifiable claims should be removed or corrected.
Section 7 – Technical infrastructure & archiving
34. Articles have persistent identifiers (e.g. DOIs, Handles).
Recommended
Each research article has a unique persistent identifier (such as a DOI) visible on the article page and/or PDF, and the identifier resolves correctly when followed.
35. The archiving policy on the website matches the Keepers record at portal.issn.org.
Recommended
The website states where content is digitally preserved (e.g. LOCKSS, CLOCKSS, Portico, national library), and the same service(s) appear in the Keepers section of the journal’s record at portal.issn.org, or you have a documented, official agreement with the service provider.
36. The journal states a clear self-archiving / repository policy.
Recommended
Authors can find information on whether they may deposit preprints, accepted manuscripts, and/or published versions in repositories (institutional or disciplinary), and under what conditions.
